# Product Launch Formula-inspired Codex adoption strategy

This document adapts the educational launch logic associated with Jeff Walker's Product Launch Formula to a modern SaaS and agent-adoption context. It is an independent interpretation, not an official Jeff Walker or OpenAI program.

## Strategic objective

Move a defined audience through four states:

```text
Curious about agents
    ↓
Understands a valuable opportunity
    ↓
Sees a believable transformation
    ↓
Experiences a first successful workflow
    ↓
Chooses sustainable paid usage based on evidence
```

The offer is not merely a subscription. The offer is a guided transition from chat-based AI use to supervised delegation of real work.

## Audience

### Primary pilot segment

QA engineers, QA leads, product managers, and system/product analysts who:

- repeat evidence-heavy workflows;
- spend several hours per week on investigation, test design, reporting, or backlog preparation;
- already use AI intermittently but do not yet operate a repeatable agent workflow;
- can bring a real but non-sensitive project to the pilot.

### Exclusions for the first cohort

- users needing production access to highly sensitive systems;
- users expecting fully autonomous high-impact actions;
- participants without a real workflow to test;
- participants who cannot measure a baseline or review output quality.

## Phase 0 — Pre-prelaunch research

### Core research question

> Which recurring work item consumes the most professional attention while producing an output that can be reviewed objectively?

### Research assets

- five-question role survey;
- ten short problem interviews;
- task-frequency and pain matrix;
- current plan and Codex-use snapshot;
- objections log;
- language bank made from the audience's own wording.

### Required output

Select one primary workflow and one backup workflow using:

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Frequency | 20% |
| Human time consumed | 20% |
| Reviewability of output | 20% |
| Safety and boundedness | 15% |
| Applicability across the segment | 15% |
| Likelihood of repeat use | 10% |

The first proposed workflow is **QA and Product Audit**.

## Education sequence

### Event 1 — Opportunity

**Working title:** From asking AI questions to delegating inspectable work

#### Belief to create

> Agentic work is a new operating model, not merely a faster chat interface.

#### Content

- difference between answers and delegated workflows;
- why long-running and parallel tasks change the economics of knowledge work;
- examples of bounded QA, analysis, and product tasks;
- what humans must continue to own;
- a self-assessment: “What should I delegate first?”

#### Call to action

Choose one recurring workflow and record the current human baseline.

### Event 2 — Transformation

**Working title:** One professional, a supervised team of agents

#### Belief to create

> A small team can increase scope without surrendering judgment or control.

#### Demonstration narrative

```text
Raw product or repository context
    ↓
Agent 1: requirements and journey map
Agent 2: risk and test model
Agent 3: evidence collection
Agent 4: product and business impact
    ↓
Human review and correction
    ↓
Accepted audit package
```

#### Proof design

Show:

- original input and boundaries;
- prompts and task decomposition;
- intermediate artifacts;
- rejected or corrected output;
- final accepted package;
- elapsed time and human review time;
- limitations and unresolved questions.

The demonstration must expose the review process. A polished result without visible evidence would weaken trust.

#### Call to action

Map the participant's workflow into agent tasks, human approvals, and acceptance criteria.

### Event 3 — Ownership experience

**Working title:** Your first ten hours back

#### Belief to create

> I can see how this would work in my own week, with my own controls and economics.

#### Assets

- first-workflow template;
- plan-decision worksheet;
- ROI calculator;
- safe delegation checklist;
- review rubric;
- sample outcome receipt;
- seven-day calendar.

#### Call to action

Join the bounded Build Week cohort and complete one workflow.

## Enrollment window — Codex Build Week

The window is limited only by real operating capacity: live onboarding, artifact review, and measurement support.

### Seven-day participant journey

| Day | Participant outcome |
|---|---|
| 0 | Baseline, consent, workflow boundary, and success criteria recorded |
| 1 | Environment and context prepared; first task delegated |
| 2 | First inspectable artifact produced |
| 3 | Parallel or staged task decomposition tested |
| 4 | Human review, corrections, and safety boundary documented |
| 5 | Final artifact accepted or explicitly rejected |
| 6 | Time, quality, cost, and repeatability measured |
| 7 | Next workflow and sustainable plan/API path selected |

## Modern launch mechanisms

### 1. Behavior-triggered education

Messages should respond to actual progress rather than a fixed email schedule alone:

- selected workflow but did not start;
- started but no artifact produced;
- artifact produced but not reviewed;
- workflow accepted but no repeat plan;
- included limits reached after proven value.

### 2. Outcome receipt

At workflow completion, produce a compact record:

```text
Workflow completed
Artifacts accepted: 4/5
Human baseline: 6.0 hours
Human review: 1.4 hours
Estimated time returned: 4.6 hours
Known limitations: 3
Suggested next workflow: regression evidence pack
Recommended commercial path: continue current plan / evaluate higher usage / evaluate API
```

All time savings must be labelled as measured, estimated, or self-reported.

### 3. Mobile continuity

Use the mobile Codex experience as part of the narrative:

- review progress away from the desk;
- answer clarification questions;
- approve bounded actions;
- inspect screenshots, diffs, and tests;
- prevent long-running work from stalling.

The message is continuity and supervision, not “work all the time.”

### 4. Community proof

Collect structured evidence rather than generic testimonials:

- role and workflow;
- baseline;
- accepted artifact;
- review burden;
- result after seven and thirty days;
- plan decision;
- permission level for public use.

### 5. Seed launch to evergreen system

```text
Live seed cohort
    ↓
Objection and failure analysis
    ↓
Revised workflow kits
    ↓
Second measured cohort
    ↓
Segment-specific evergreen onboarding
    ↓
Partner or co-sell proposal
```

Do not automate before the failure modes are understood.

## Offer architecture

### Core offer

A seven-day guided implementation of one evidence-backed Codex workflow.

### Deliverables

- workflow map;
- context pack;
- agent task decomposition;
- human approval map;
- final artifact package;
- outcome receipt;
- plan/API decision memo;
- thirty-day follow-up measurement.

### Optional bonuses

Bonuses should remove implementation friction rather than inflate perceived value:

- QA audit prompt pack;
- product discovery prompt pack;
- agent review rubric;
- cost-per-workflow calculator;
- manager-ready business case template.

## Objection map

| Objection | Response mechanism |
|---|---|
| “I do not know what to delegate.” | Role-specific workflow selector |
| “It will create more review work.” | Review-burden measurement and acceptance rubric |
| “I cannot trust the result.” | Evidence links, rejected-output examples, and human approval map |
| “I do not understand the plans.” | Frequency, concurrency, and cost-based plan worksheet |
| “Tokens and credits are unpredictable.” | Cost-per-accepted-workflow reporting |
| “This will replace my role.” | Position the participant as workflow owner, reviewer, and decision-maker |
| “My data is sensitive.” | Explicit eligibility and data-handling gate before participation |

## Launch scorecard

The launch is successful only if it creates durable behavior, not merely registrations.

### Primary metrics

- eligible participant activation rate;
- first accepted artifact rate;
- median time to first value;
- seven-day repeat workflow rate;
- thirty-day retained use;
- verified or self-reported hours returned;
- review-to-output ratio;
- plan-selection confidence;
- attributable paid conversion or expansion, where measurement is contractually available.

### Guardrail metrics

- output rejection rate;
- material factual error rate;
- unsafe or unauthorized action attempts;
- participant confusion about independence or OpenAI endorsement;
- support time per activated participant;
- refund, complaint, or dropout reasons.

## Ethical launch rules

- no invented deadlines;
- no fabricated testimonials;
- no promise of guaranteed savings or income;
- no claim of OpenAI affiliation without written approval;
- no plan recommendation without a workflow and usage basis;
- no revenue claim without an agreed attribution method;
- no concealment of failed or rejected agent output.

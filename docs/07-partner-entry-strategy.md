# Partner entry strategy

**Snapshot date:** 2026-07-21  
**Status:** internal strategy; not an OpenAI program commitment.

## Objective

Convert the Codex Adoption Lab from an independent concept into a credible delivery asset without overstating current scale, references, or partner eligibility.

## Reality check

The public OpenAI Partner Network emphasizes:

- measurable customer outcomes;
- repeatable use-case identification;
- workflow redesign and integration;
- adoption and change management;
- industry expertise and customer relationships;
- technical and deployment capability;
- co-sell engagement and sales performance;
- reliable delivery capacity.

The Lab currently has strategy assets and a pilot protocol, but not yet an external delivery record, validated customer outcomes, or attributable revenue evidence.

Therefore, a direct revenue-share request to OpenAI is not the first move.

## Two-path strategy

### Path A — co-delivery with an existing OpenAI partner

**Recommended near-term path.**

Approach a listed Partner Network organization whose offer already includes AI adoption, engineering, product transformation, QA modernization, or change management.

Proposed contribution:

- access to a focused QA/Product professional segment;
- runnable first-workflow selection and measurement assets;
- evidence and acceptance framework;
- transparent failure reporting;
- cohort facilitation;
- outcome receipts and retention measurement.

Partner contribution:

- customer access and qualification;
- contracting and procurement;
- security and data-governance review;
- delivery oversight;
- brand and OpenAI relationship;
- commercial attribution where available.

Initial ask:

> Run one bounded co-delivery experiment for 5–10 qualified participants, with fixed scope, no public OpenAI affiliation claim, and a pre-agreed evidence report.

Commercial starting point:

- fixed delivery fee or subcontractor fee;
- optional capped success bonus for accepted workflow and retained use;
- no revenue share until attribution data and contracting support exist.

### Path B — direct OpenAI Partner Network application

**Recommended after evidence maturity improves.**

Minimum internal readiness target:

1. five completed real workflows;
2. at least three accepted or partially accepted outcomes;
3. documented failures and process revisions;
4. day-7 and day-30 follow-up;
5. two or more independent participant references where permission exists;
6. stable delivery assets and data-handling process;
7. one clear industry or functional wedge;
8. a credible customer acquisition or community channel;
9. operating capacity beyond one improvised engagement;
10. no unresolved critical safety or trust issue.

Application narrative:

- problem: professionals struggle to convert Codex capability into repeatable, measurable workflows;
- wedge: QA, product, and analysis evidence workflows;
- method: bounded adoption sequence with human gates and acceptance criteria;
- proof: completed cases, failures, retention, facilitator effort, and participant judgment;
- commercial model: fixed or hybrid pilot before any attributed revenue arrangement;
- expansion: role-specific workflow kits and partner-led delivery.

## Qualification questions for a potential partner

1. Do you currently deliver Codex, ChatGPT Work, or agentic-workflow adoption programs?
2. Which customer segment has an unresolved activation or measurement problem?
3. Can you support participant data, security, and consent requirements?
4. Can the parties agree on a source of truth for activation and retention?
5. Who owns delivery quality and consequential decisions?
6. What public claims and brand references are permitted?
7. Is the pilot paid, subsidized, or exploratory?
8. What would justify a second cohort?

## Stop conditions

Do not proceed when:

- the partner expects fabricated urgency, testimonials, or ROI;
- participant data rules are undefined;
- the Lab is asked to imply OpenAI endorsement;
- output acceptance is controlled by the facilitator rather than a qualified reviewer;
- revenue attribution is added after a campaign begins;
- production access or consequential autonomy exceeds the approved boundary;
- unpaid scope is materially larger than the evidence value of the experiment.

## External positioning rule

Do not lead with “Jeff Walker PLF,” “affiliate,” or “revenue share.” Lead with:

> A measured activation and workflow-adoption system for QA and product teams, with human acceptance, cost-per-accepted-workflow reporting, and transparent failure evidence.

The educational launch design may remain an internal method. The buyer-facing offer is measurable adoption and delivery reliability.

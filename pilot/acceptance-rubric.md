## 1. Scoring

Score each dimension from 0 to 4.

- `0` — absent, unusable, or materially misleading
- `1` — major deficiencies; extensive replacement required
- `2` — partially useful; substantial correction required
- `3` — useful with limited correction
- `4` — ready for the agreed purpose with no material correction

Weighted result: `sum(score × weight)`, producing a value from 0 to 4.

## 2. Mandatory gates

Regardless of weighted score, the result cannot be fully accepted when any of the following is true:

- a material claim lacks evidence and is not labeled as inference;
- a confirmed finding is not reproducible from the recorded state;
- a known critical limitation is hidden;
- prohibited data or an unauthorized action was used;
- the artifact implies authority the agent did not have;
- a major rejected hypothesis is presented as fact;
- the reviewer is not qualified to evaluate the main result.

## 3. Acceptance statuses

### Accepted

Use when:

- weighted score is at least 3.2;
- all mandatory gates pass;
- no material replacement work is required;
- the output can be used for the agreed purpose.

### Partially accepted

Use when:

- weighted score is at least 2.0;
- mandatory safety and evidence gates pass;
- part of the output is useful;
- corrections, narrowing, or additional human work are required.

Record exactly which sections are accepted and which are rejected.

### Rejected

Use when:

- weighted score is below 2.0; or
- any mandatory gate fails materially; or
- the output does not support the agreed decision or artifact.

Rejection is a valid pilot result and must remain in the evidence set.

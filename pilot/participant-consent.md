## Purpose

The Codex Adoption Lab seed pilot evaluates whether one supervised Codex workflow can produce a useful, human-reviewed outcome. The pilot is independent and is not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by OpenAI.

## What may be collected

- role and workflow description;
- non-sensitive or sanitized source material;
- task and artifact timestamps;
- setup, review, and correction time;
- accepted, partial, and rejected outputs;
- failures and boundary incidents;
- participant feedback at completion, day 7, and day 30;
- voluntary plan or usage information.

## What must not be provided

- passwords, tokens, private keys, or credentials;
- production secrets;
- regulated personal, health, financial, or government data unless a separately approved process exists;
- third-party confidential information without permission;
- material the participant is not authorized to share;
- data whose storage, processing, or disclosure requirements are unknown.

## Participation choices

The participant may:

- stop the case at any time;
- decline to answer optional questions;
- reject any finding or artifact;
- prohibit publication of their identity, company, workflow, or result;
- request deletion of pilot-held identifiable case material, subject to already published aggregate or legally required records;
- decline any purchase, upgrade, or commercial recommendation.
